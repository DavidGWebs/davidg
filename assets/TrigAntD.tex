\section*{One of these Guys is a Trig Guy...}
\textbf{INSTRUCTIONS:} Today's worksheet should give you the heuristics/intuition to pick out whether a derivative has a trigonometric anti-derivative (or not). The problems come in pairs, one will be a trig anti-derivative, the other will not be. Compute both. (HINT: You can verify your answer by taking the derivative).
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Pair 1:
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item $f'(x) = \frac{2x}{\sqrt{1-x^4}}$
        \vfill
        \item $g'(x) = -\frac{2x}{(1+x^2)^2}$
        \vfill
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Pair 2:
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item $f'(x) = \frac{1}{(1+x^2)+1}$
        \vfill
        \item $g'(x) = -\frac{2}{(2x-1)^2}$
        \vfill
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Pair 3 (HARD):
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item $f'(x) = \frac{1}{2(\sqrt{x}+1)^2\sqrt{x}}$
        \vfill
        \item $g'(x) = -\frac{1}{2\sqrt{-x^2+x}}$
        \vfill
    \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}
